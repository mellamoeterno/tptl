React.cloneElement()


take the existing props a child already has, and add new props to them or overwrite some of them 
— but keep the child’s original type (like <button> or <MyComponent>) the same.

cloneElement() takes an existing React element and makes a copy of it. 
On that copy, you can change or add new props, and even change its children


The "augment" thing (adding/modifying props) works with React.cloneElement() 
by itself. React.Children.map() is just a common place to use it, not a requirement.
